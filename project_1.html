Project Overview

  • Significance:
    • Event: ABU Robocon 2023 organized by the Asia-Pacific Broadcasting Union.
    • Team: Represented Kumaraguru College of Technology as part of Team iKon from iQube.
  • Project Theme:
    • Objective: Casting Flowers Over Angkor Wat - Build robots to replicate the act of offering flowers (rings) at the iconic Angkor Wat temple complex.
    • Robots: Designed and constructed two robots - a "rabbit" robot and an "elephant" robot.
    • Task: Throw rings onto designated poles within the game field.
  • My Contributions:
    • Coding: Developed coding logic to control robots' movements and actions.
    • Control Systems: Designed closed-loop control systems using motor encoders for accurate movement.
    • Hardware Selection: Assisted in selecting various motors and actuators:
      • Planetary geared DC motors for locomotion.
      • Stepper motors for ring lifting.
      • Servo motors for holding rings.
      • Linear actuators for adjusting the shooting mechanism angles.
    • Power Distribution: Designed and implemented the power distribution system for efficient power supply to various components of the robots.
  • Achievements:
    • Stages: Successfully completed Stage 1 and Stage 2, and participated in Stage 3 of national level DD Robocon 2023 at the Thyagaraja Sports Complex, New Delhi.
    • Outcome: Demonstrated dedication, collaborative spirit, and honed invaluable skills throughout the project.
  • Personal Growth:
    • Experience: Enriched and transformed my knowledge and deepened the understanding of robotics.
    • Skills: Enhanced teamwork, problem-solving, and critical thinking abilities.
    • Technical Expertise: Developed skills in embedded systems and electrical engineering.
    • Satisfaction: Witnessing our robots' performance and contributing to a shared goal was truly rewarding.
