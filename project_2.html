Equinox

  • Project website: Cells Monitoring website
  • Project Application: Equinox
  • Technologies used:
    1. Converter designing softwares - MATLAB

    6. Internet of Things - HTTPS

Project Overview

  • Significance:
    • Lithium-ion batteries play a crucial role in everyday life.
    • Battery packs consist of multiple cells connected in series and parallel.
    • Improper usage and aging cause cell imbalance, leading to overheating and reduced performance.
  • Proposed Technique:
    • Active cell balancing using a Bidirectional Flyback current-fed Cuk converter.
    • Selective balancing algorithm to minimize balancing cycles for a 14V 3000mAh Lithium-ion battery.
  • Process:
    • Continuously monitor State of Charge (SOC) levels to identify weaker cells.
    • Charge weaker cells to balance SOC levels using the proposed converters.
    • Flyback section ensures isolation and seamless power transfer.
    • Cuk converter enhances efficiency and voltage transformation.
  • Benefits:
    • Improved battery lifetime and overall efficiency.
    • Enhanced life cycles of cells through effective charging and balancing.
    • Continuously monitoring the parameters of the cells via ThinkSpeak IoT platform through a website (Cells Monitor) and a Mobile Application (Equinox).
